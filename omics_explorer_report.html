Protein NPX Overview

ⓘ  What does this mean?

Left panel — combined NPX distribution: All 1005 proteins stacked into one histogram. NPX (Normalized Protein eXpression) is Olink's measurement unit. It is log2-normalised: a difference of 1 NPX = 2x difference in protein abundance. Typical NPX values range from roughly -5 to +15. A left-skewed distribution (tail toward low values) is expected for Olink data because low-abundance proteins cluster near the limit of detection. The dashed line marks the mean across all measurements.

Right panel — missingness per protein: Each bar is one protein and the percentage of participants where that protein was undetectable (below the limit of detection). The orange line at 5% is the MEDIUM warning threshold; red at 20% is HIGH. Proteins with high missingness are unreliable biomarker candidates. For STARNET Target96 data after QC, most proteins should sit near zero.

Individual Protein Distributions

ⓘ  What does this mean?

Each subplot shows the NPX distribution for one protein across all 532 participants. Coral = no quality warnings, salmon/red = at least one flag. Shapes to look for: bell-shaped = healthy distribution; spike at a single value = Olink LOD floor artifact (many samples imputed to the same floor); extreme right tail = outlier driving up the z-score; bimodal (two peaks) = possible batch effect or two biological sub-populations. Only the first 20 proteins are shown here. The full quality scan covers all 1005 proteins and is recorded in the warning table and JSON export below.

Quality Warnings (536 total)

ⓘ  What does this mean?

Every quality issue detected across all columns, with severity and detail. Flag types:
EXTREME_OUTLIER — a value more than 4 SD from the mean (possible batch artifact or pipetting error);
SUSPECTED_LOD_FLOOR — more than 30% of values are identical, suggesting Olink imputed a limit-of-detection floor (protein not reliably detected);
HIGH_MISSINGNESS / MODERATE_MISSINGNESS — too many missing values;
HIGH_SKEWNESS — highly asymmetric distribution (may need log-transform before modelling);
LOW_VARIANCE — almost no variation across samples (useless as a biomarker);
DUPLICATE_ROWS — identical rows exist (data loading or merging error).
